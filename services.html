The Process

Four Stages To Clarity

Every restoration follows the same proven sequence — no shortcuts, no guesswork.

1

Inspection & Assessment

We examine each lens for depth of oxidation, micro-cracking, and moisture intrusion. This tells us exactly how many sanding stages your headlights need and whether any lens damage is beyond restoration — so you never pay for a service that won't deliver results.

2

Multi-Stage Sanding

Starting with heavy-grit paper and stepping down through progressively finer grits, we remove the oxidized, yellowed layer of polycarbonate. This strips away the damage at the source — not just the surface haze — restoring true optical clarity to the lens.

3

Polishing & Refinement

A machine-polishing stage with fine compound brings the lens to a flawless, glass-like finish. This is where the headlight goes from "clear" to "showroom new" — the kind of clarity that makes a noticeable difference in nighttime visibility.

4

UV-Protective Sealant

The most important step. We apply an industrial-grade UV-inhibiting ceramic sealant that bonds to the lens surface. This is what keeps your headlights clear for years instead of months — blocking the same UV rays that fogged them up originally.

5

Final Inspection

We photograph the finished result, check light output, and confirm the sealant has cured evenly. You see the before-and-after before we pack up — and we only leave when you're satisfied with the clarity.

Why It Matters

More Than Just Looks

Restoring your headlights is one of the highest-ROI maintenance items on your vehicle.

01

Clarity Restored

Yellowed, hazy lenses cut your light output by up to 80%. Restoration returns that clarity — you'll see the road the way your car was designed to show it.

02

Nighttime Safety

Clear headlights mean a wider, brighter beam pattern. That's earlier hazard detection, better reaction time, and safer driving for you and everyone on the road.

03

UV Longevity

Our sealant doesn't just make them look good today — it blocks the UV that caused the damage. Treated lenses stay clear far longer than unsealed ones.

04

Cost vs. Replacement

A new headlight assembly can run $300–$1,200 per unit. Restoration delivers the same visual result at a fraction of the cost — and we come to you.
